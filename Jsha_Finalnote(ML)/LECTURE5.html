Topic: Example Probability Distributions (Discrete and Continuous)

üìù One-Liner Notes

Discrete Random Variables

  • The **Bernoulli distribution** models a random variable with only two possible outcomes: success (1) or failure (0).
  • The **Binomial distribution** generalizes the Bernoulli distribution to $n$ independent and identical trials.
  • The **Poisson distribution** models the number of events occurring in a fixed interval of time or space.
  • The **Geometric distribution** models the number of Bernoulli trials needed to get the **first** success.

Continuous Random Variables

  • The **Uniform distribution** models a continuous random variable that has an equal probability to lie anywhere in a specific domain $x \in (a, b)$.
  • The **Normal/Gaussian distribution** is commonly known as the "**bell curve**".
  • The **Central Limit Theorem** states that the normalized sum of many independent and identical distributions tends to the normal distribution.
  • The **Exponential distribution** represents the probability underlying the **time interval** between two incidences of an event.
  • The **Student's t distribution** is used for determining **confidence intervals** for a range in which the true fit lies, especially in linear regression.

‚úÖ Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

**MCQ 1: Which probability distribution models the number of independent events occurring in a fixed interval at a constant average rate $\lambda$?**

A. Geometric distribution B. Binomial distribution C. Poisson distribution D. Uniform distribution
Answer: C. The Poisson distribution specifically models the number of independent events over a fixed interval, characterized by the average rate parameter $\lambda$.

**MCQ 2: What is the defining characteristic of the Probability Density Function (PDF) for a Uniform distribution over the range $[a, b]$?**

A. It is proportional to $e^{-x}$ B. It is $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma}$ C. It is $\frac{1}{b-a}$ for $a \le x \le b$ D. It is $\binom{n}{k}p^k(1-p)^{n-k}$
Answer: C. For a Uniform distribution, the PDF $f(x)$ is constant and equal to $1/(b-a)$ over the defined domain $[a, b]$, ensuring the total area is 1.

**MCQ 3: The **Binomial distribution** generalizes which other distribution to $n$ independent trials?**

A. Poisson distribution B. Normal distribution C. Geometric distribution D. Bernoulli distribution
Answer: D. The Binomial distribution counts the number of successes in $n$ repeated, independent, and identical Bernoulli trials.

‚ùì Short Question & Answer

**Q1: What is the core difference between the Bernoulli and the Binomial distributions?**

**A:** The **Bernoulli distribution** models a single trial with two outcomes (success/failure). The **Binomial distribution** models the number of successes, $k$, in a fixed number of $n$ independent and identical Bernoulli trials.

**Q2: What is the common name for the Normal/Gaussian distribution and why is it important in statistics?**

**A:** It is commonly known as the "**bell curve**". It is fundamentally important due to the **Central Limit Theorem**, which states that the sum of many independent random variables, regardless of their original distribution, tends toward a normal distribution.

**Q3: State the condition and PMF for the Geometric distribution.**

**A:** The Geometric distribution models the number of Bernoulli trials, $k$, required to achieve the **first success**.
The **Probability Mass Function (PMF)** is: $$P(X=k)=(1-p)^{k-1}p \quad \text{for } k=1, 2, 3, \ldots$$

üìö Long Q&A / Description / Graphical Questions

**Q1: Write the Probability Density Function (PDF) for the Normal/Gaussian distribution and explain the roles of its two parameters.**

**A:** The PDF for a Normal/Gaussian distribution $X \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)$ is: $$f_{x}(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma} \exp\left(-\frac{(x-\mu)^{2}}{2\sigma^{2}}\right)$$ The two parameters are:

  1. **Mean ($\mu$):** Represents the **center** of the distribution. The curve is symmetric around $x = \mu$.
  2. **Standard Deviation ($\sigma$):** Represents the **spread** or dispersion of the distribution. It is a measure of the extent of deviation from the mean. (The variance is $\sigma^2$).

**Q2: Describe the key features and PDF of the Uniform distribution for a continuous random variable $X$.**

**A:** The Uniform distribution models a continuous random variable $X$ that has an **equal probability** of lying anywhere within a defined domain, $x \in (a, b)$. It has no peaks or troughs over this interval.
The **Probability Density Function (PDF)** is defined by: $$f(x)=\begin{cases}\frac{1}{b-a} & a \le x \le b \\ 0 & x < a \text{ or } x > b\end{cases}$$ The height of the PDF, $\frac{1}{b-a}$, ensures that the area of the rectangle (base $\times$ height) over the interval $[a, b]$ is exactly 1, satisfying the total probability rule.
