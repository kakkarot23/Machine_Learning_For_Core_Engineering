üìù One-Liner Notes

Random Variables and Types

  • A **Random Variable (RV)** is a variable that denotes the outcome of a stochastic experiment.
  • The two main types of Random Variables are **Discrete** and **Continuous**.
  • Two main functions that give probability distribution for an RV are the **Probability Density Function (PDF)** and the **Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF)**.

PDF and CDF Fundamentals

  • For a continuous RV, the probability of $X$ lying in a small interval $dx$ is $P(x \le X \le x+dx) = f(x) \cdot dx$, where $f(x)$ is the **PDF**.
  • The **CDF**, $F(x)$, gives the probability that $X$ has a value **less than or equal to** $x$.
  • The total probability over the entire range, $F(\infty)$, must always equal **1** ($\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x)dx = 1$).
  • The probability of $X$ lying between two values $a$ and $b$ is $P(a \le X \le b) = F(b) - F(a)$.

Multiple Random Variables

  • The distribution of multiple RVs is defined by the **Joint Probability Density Function (JPDF)**, $f(x, y)$.
  • If two random variables $X$ and $Y$ are **independent**, their JPDF is the product of their individual PDFs: $f_{XY}(x, y) = f_X(x)f_Y(y)$.

**MCQ 1: Which formula correctly represents the calculation of the Posterior Probability using Bayes Theorem?**

A. $P(A_i|B) = \frac{P(A_i) + P(B|A_i)}{P(B)}$ B. $P(A_i|B) = \frac{P(A_i)P(B|A_i)}{\sum_{j}P(A_j) \cdot P(B|A_j)}$ C. $P(A_i|B) = P(A_i)P(B|A_i)P(B)$ D. $P(A_i|B) = \frac{P(A_i|B)P(A_i)}{P(B)}$
Answer: B. This is the general form of Bayes Theorem where $P(B)$ in the denominator is expanded as the sum of joint probabilities over all possible hypotheses $A_j$.

**MCQ 2: What is the numerical value of the total area under the Probability Density Function (PDF) curve, $\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x) dx$?**

A. 0 B. $\infty$ C. 1 D. $F(x)$
Answer: C. $F(\infty) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x)dx = 1$. The total probability of a random variable $X$ taking any value in its domain ($\mathbb{R}$) is 1.

‚ùì Short Question & Answer

**Q1: Define a Random Variable (RV) and name the two primary categories.**

**A:** A **Random Variable** is a variable that denotes the outcome of a stochastic (random) experiment. The two primary categories of random variables are **Discrete** (takes on countable values) and **Continuous** (takes on any value within a given range).

**Q2: How is the probability of a continuous RV, $X$, lying between two values $a$ and $b$ calculated using its PDF, $f(x)$?**

**A:** The probability $P(a \le X \le b)$ is calculated by integrating the Probability Density Function $f(x)$ over the interval $[a, b]$: $$P(a \le X \le b) = \int_{a}^{b} f(x) dx$$

**Q3: What condition must be satisfied by the JPDF for two random variables, $X$ and $Y$, to be considered statistically independent?**

**A:** For $X$ and $Y$ to be independent, their Joint Probability Density Function (JPDF) must be equal to the product of their individual (marginal) PDFs: $$f_{XY}(x, y) = f_X(x) f_Y(y)$$

**Q1: Apply Bayes Theorem to the following problem: In an ML class, 40% of students are from Science and 60% from Engineering. 20% of Science students and 30% of Engineering students have prior ML exposure. Find the probability of a student being from Science, given they have ML exposure.**

**A:** This requires finding $P(\text{Science}|\text{ML Exposure})$. Let $A_1 = \text{Science}$, $A_2 = \text{Engineering}$, and $B = \text{ML Exposure}$.

  1. **Given Probabilities (Priors and Likelihoods):**
    • $P(A_1) = 0.4$ (Prior probability of Science)
    • $P(A_2) = 0.6$ (Prior probability of Engineering)
    • $P(B|A_1) = 0.2$ (Likelihood of ML exposure, given Science)
    • $P(B|A_2) = 0.3$ (Likelihood of ML exposure, given Engineering)
  2. **Bayes Theorem Formula:** $$P(A_1|B) = \frac{P(A_1)P(B|A_1)}{P(A_1)P(B|A_1) + P(A_2)P(B|A_2)}$$
  3. **Calculation:** $$P(\text{Science}|\text{ML}) = \frac{0.4 \times 0.2}{(0.4 \times 0.2) + (0.6 \times 0.3)} = \frac{0.08}{0.08 + 0.18} = \frac{0.08}{0.26}$$
  4. **Final Result:** $$P(\text{Science}|\text{ML Exposure}) \approx 0.3077$$ The probability of a randomly chosen student being from a Science background, given they have prior ML exposure, is approximately $30.77\%$.

**Q2: Explain the concepts of Probability Density Function (PDF) and Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF) for continuous random variables and show their fundamental mathematical relationship.**

**A:**

Probability Density Function (PDF), $f(x)$:
The PDF describes the **relative likelihood** for a continuous random variable (RV) to take on a given value. For a continuous RV, the probability of taking *exactly* one value is zero; hence, the PDF is used to find the probability of the RV falling within a small range $dx$, given by $f(x) \cdot dx$. The total area under the PDF curve must equal 1, representing the certainty that $X$ will take some value ($\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x) dx = 1$).
Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF), $F(x)$:
The CDF gives the probability that the continuous RV $X$ takes a value **less than or equal to** a specific argument $x$. It accumulates the probability density from $-\infty$ up to $x$.
Mathematical Relationship:
The CDF is the integral of the PDF, and conversely, the PDF is the first derivative of the CDF:
$$F(x) = P(-\infty < X \le x) = \int_{-\infty}^{x} f(x') dx'$$ $$f(x) = \frac{dF(x)}{dx}$$
