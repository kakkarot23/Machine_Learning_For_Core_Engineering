Core Concepts

  • **Data Science** is the application of statistical and mathematical principles to analyze and understand data.
  • **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** involves imparting human-like cognitive functions to machines for inference and decision-making.
  • **Machine Learning (ML)** is the use of computers to learn from datasets to make predictions for unseen data points, rather than just memorizing.
  • **The process of ML** includes Model Selection, Prediction, Data Generation, Feature Engineering, and Training.

History of AI/ML

  • **1950** saw Alan Turing propose the **Turing Test**.
  • The term **AI** was coined in **1956** at the Dartmouth workshop.
  • **Arthur Samuel** defined ML in **1959** as the ability of a computer to learn without being explicitly programmed.
  • The **1980s-1990s** saw the rise of ML, shifting from rules to **data-driven learning**.
  • The **Deep Learning revolution** took place from the **2000s-2020s**, fueled by big data and GPU computing.

**MCQ 1: What is the primary role of probability and statistics in data science?**

A. To define the ethical guidelines for AI development. B. To analyze and understand data via mathematical principles. C. To completely eliminate the stochastic nature of physical processes. D. To serve as the only component of Machine Learning.
Answer: B. Probability and statistics are crucial because they are the statistical and mathematical principles applied to **analyze and understand data**. They play a major role due to the **probabilistic (stochastic) nature** of many real-world processes.

**MCQ 2: Which of the following is an advantage of using Machine Learning?**

A. It always provides models that are fully interpretable in terms of physics/chemistry. B. It requires very little data to train highly accurate models. C. It can reduce the computational cost of making predictions after training. D. It eliminates the need for any linear algebra or multivariate optimization.
Answer: C. A significant advantage of ML is that once a model is trained, it can **reduce the computational cost of making predictions** by several orders of magnitude. Requiring less data and obscuring physics are generally disadvantages, not advantages.

**MCQ 3: In the context of the relationship between AI and ML, which statement is accurate?**

A. AI is a subset of ML, focusing only on predictions. B. ML is a subset of AI, where machines make predictions or learn patterns. C. AI and ML are completely separate fields with no overlap. D. Both AI and ML exclusively focus on symbolic, rule-based reasoning.
Answer: B. The diagram shows that **ML is contained within AI**. ML focuses on machines making **predictions or learning patterns**, while the broader AI involves machines making **decisions and/or taking actions**.

‚ùì Short Question & Answer

**Q1: What is the definition of Machine Learning (ML)?**

**A:** Machine learning is the use of computers to **learn** (rather than memorize) from datasets involving a number of variables, enabling them to make **predictions** corresponding to unseen data points.

**Q2: List the three main reasons for the increasing popularity of AI and ML in core engineering.**

**A:** The popularity is surging due to:

  1. **Availability of large amounts of data** (even with variable quality).
  2. **Development of new and advanced algorithms** for understanding and predicting data.
  3. **Ability to automate experiments and simulations**, along with the rise of field-specific large-language models.

**Q3: What role does Feature Engineering play in the ML process?**

**A:** Feature Engineering is one of the five key stages in the machine learning process. It precedes the Training stage and is crucial for preparing the input data in a way that allows the learning algorithm to perform optimally.

**Q1: Describe the key advantages and disadvantages of Machine Learning.**

Advantages:

  • **Mitigates challenges** associated with a lack of closed-form expressions or theories for a process.
  • Helps **demystify datasets with a high degree of dimensionality** (large number of independent variables) where individual effects are unclear.
  • **Reduces computational cost** of making predictions by several orders of magnitude once the model is trained.

Disadvantages:

  • Models typically require a **large amount of data** to train, which may not always be available or may be of poor quality. (This is mitigated by foundation models and fine-tuning strategies).
  • ML models may **obscure the physics, chemistry, or biology** of the underlying problem because all the focus is on data. (This is being addressed by physics-informed or physics-inspired ML).

**Q2: Illustrate the relationship between AI and ML and list the mathematical fundamentals that underpin ML.**

**A:** Machine Learning (ML) is a **subset of Artificial Intelligence (AI)**.

  • **ML (Inner Circle):** Machines primarily make **predictions or learn patterns**.
  • **AI (Outer Circle):** Machines make **decisions and/or take actions**, encompassing ML's capabilities and more.
**Mathematical Fundamentals for ML:**

The ML subset is underpinned by several mathematical and statistical fields:

  1. **Probability and statistics**
  2. **Linear algebra**
  3. **Multivariate optimization**

**Q3: Provide an example of ML application for each of the core engineering disciplines listed in the notes.**

**A:** Machine Learning has diverse applications across core engineering disciplines:

  • **Chemical Engineering:** Optimization of operating conditions for a chemical plant to increase process efficiency.
  • **Materials Engineering:** Discovery of new electrocatalysts for green hydrogen production.
  • **Electrical Engineering:** Circuit design for reducing power consumption in processors.
  • **Civil Engineering:** Optimizing structural layouts of skyscrapers for earthquake resistance.
  • **Mechanical/Aerospace Engineering:** Automobile or aeroplane design for increased fuel efficiency.
